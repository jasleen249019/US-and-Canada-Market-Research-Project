# US-and-Canada-Market-Research-Project


## Project Overview

This project analyzes the Data Analytics and AI job markets across the United States and Canada to uncover hiring trends, salary insights, work mode distribution, and market opportunities. The objective was to provide data-driven business recommendations to support ACC's expansion into the US and Canadian markets through analytics training, placement services, and business intelligence solutions.

## Objectives

* Analyze hiring trends in the US and Canada analytics market.
* Compare salary patterns across job roles, cities, and countries.
* Study remote vs. on-site job opportunities.
* Identify business opportunities for analytics training and consulting.
* Provide strategic recommendations for market expansion.

## Tools & Technologies

* **Power BI** – Interactive dashboards and data visualization
* **Microsoft Excel** – Data cleaning, preprocessing, and transformation
* **Power Query** – Data transformation and feature engineering

## Dashboard Highlights

* Total Job Opportunities
* Average Salary Analysis
* Jobs by Country
* Salary by Job Position
* Salary by City
* Salary by Work Mode
* Salary Comparison (US vs Canada)
* Interactive slicers for Country and Work Mode

## Key Insights

* The US has significantly higher analytics hiring demand than Canada.
* Senior Data Scientist and AI-related roles offer the highest average salaries.
* Full-time positions dominate the analytics job market.
* Major technology hubs provide the highest compensation opportunities.
* Growing demand for analytics, AI, and Business Intelligence professionals presents strong expansion opportunities.

## Business Recommendations

* Prioritize expansion into the US market due to higher demand.
* Strengthen Data Analytics, AI, Python, and Power BI training programs.
* Expand placement assistance and career support services.
* Offer Business Intelligence and dashboard development solutions to organizations.
* Develop project-based learning aligned with industry requirements.
